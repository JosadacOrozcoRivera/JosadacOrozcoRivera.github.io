ARLISS Competition (2022,2023)

Black Rock Desert, NA

Description

ARLISS is a competition that takes place in Black Rock, Nevada, where university students from alrededor del mundo diseñan, construyen y lanzan prototipos de satélites. La categoría en la que participé fue la Competencia CanSat Comeback, cuyo objetivo es hacer un rover ligero y todoterreno que se lanzará desde un cohete y será expulsado a una altitud de 4 km. El robot debe desplegar un paracaídas de forma autónoma, aterrizar y recorrer el desierto hasta un lugar GPS específico.

Mechanical Design Lead

Under the engineering student group "Quantum", I contributed to the project as the mechanical design lead, organizing project timeline and tasks while onboarding 5 team members with weekly meetings and tasks to perform.

  • Design decision making for chassis geometry and materials selection.
  • Secured sponsorships and funding for the project.
  • Onboarded team members on weekly meetings.

Design & manufacture mechanical structure for rover, while taking into account strict weight and sizing limitations. Structure needed to survive a 40g acceleration, while protecting electronic components. Utilized CNC, milling and tuning to create 1060 alloy chassis.

  • Weight & size limitations were given by available payload volume of rocket and maximum launch weight.
  • 40g acceleration was exerted by payload separation explosion.

Design rover all-terrain wheels and coupling, for navigation of rough terrain in the BlackRock Desert, while taking into account the capacity to protect electronic components in a 10-foot free fall drop. Utilized CAD and laser cutter to achieve desired geometry.

  • Wheels needed to be lightweight, shock absorbent and all-terrain capable. Ethylene-vinyl acetate foam was chosen due to its properties and ease of manufacturability with laser cutter.
  • In final landing touchdown, rover would suffer a 10-foot free fall to ensure the parachute did not interfere with navigation.

Design of auto-retractable stabilizing support for rover. Rover needed to have a stabilizing support that could be retracted and auto deployed when exiting the payload bay.

Results

Rover successfully survived launch, deployment, and landing. All electronic components remained intact and wheels were able to navigate rough terrain. Below, some areas of improvement.

  • More robust navigation system to ensure rover can identify waypoints correctly.
  • Further testing of landing sequence to ensure parachute deploys before rover touchdown.
