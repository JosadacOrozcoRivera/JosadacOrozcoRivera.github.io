ARLISS Competition (2022,2023)

Black Rock Desert, NA

Description

ARLISS is a competition that takes place in Black Rock, Nevada, where university students from around the world design, build, and launch prototypes of satellites. The category where I participated was CanSat Comeback Competition, where the goal is to make a lightweight, all terrain rover that that will be launch from a rocket and will be ejected at an altitude of 4 km. The robot has to autonomously deploy a parachute, land and travel the dessert to a specific GPS location.

Mechanical Design Lead

Under the engineering student group "Quantum", I contributed to the project as the mechanical design lead, organizing project timeline and tasks while onboarding 5 team members with weekly meetings and tasks to perform.

  • Design desicion making for chasis geometry and materials selection.
  • Secured sponsorships and funding for the project.
  • Onboarded team members on weekly meetings.

Design & manufacture mechanical structure for rover, while taking into account strict weight and sizing limitations. Structure needed to survive a 40g acceleration ,while protecting electronical components. Utilized CNC, miling and tuning to create 1060 alloy chassis

Design rover all-terrain wheels and coupling, for navigation of rough terrain in the BlackRock Desert, while taking into account the capacity to protect electronical components in a 10 foot free fall drop. Utilized CAD and laser cuter to achieve desired geometry.

  • Wheels needed to be lightweight, shock absorbant and all terrian capable. Ethylene-vinyl acetate foam was chosen due to its properties and ease of manufacturability with laser cuter.
  • In final landing touchdown, rover would suffer a 10 foot free fall to make sure parachute did not interfere with navigation.

Design of auto-retractable stabilizing support for rover. Rover needed to have a stabilizing support that could be retracted and auto deployed when exiting payload bay.
