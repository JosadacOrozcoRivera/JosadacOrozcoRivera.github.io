CSU 3D-Printed Fixed-Wing Aircraft Competition (2023, 2024, 2025)

Los Angeles, CA

Description

This is an aeronautical design competition that takes place in the Cal State LA campus, where undergraduate students are challenged with designing, building and flying a 3D printed aircraft. The planes need to be lightweight, and the designs need to maximize performance given manufacturing and material constraints.

My Contribution

Team Leader

Led a team of six students from mechanical and mechatronics engineering, in organizing the research, design, manufacturing, and testing stages to make the best use of the team’s skills and time. Also focused on managing resources effectively and finding ways to promote the project to attract additional sponsors and support.

  • Coordinated project management for aircraft development.
  • Secured sponsorships and funding for the project.
  • Onboarded team members on weekly meetings.

Aicraft Dimensioning & Aerodesign

Created the first draft of how aircraft would be sized, applying aeronautical fundamentals to ensure wing geometry generated the most lift. Iterated using XFLR5 to obtain the most efficient airfoil.

  • Analyzed lift-to-drag ratio for optimal performance.
  • Tested multiple airfoil configurations in XFLR5.
  • Validated designs through simulation and prototyping.

CAD Design for Additive Manufacturing

Designed the aircraft in Solidworks, including wings, tail section, and fuselage. Due to the nature of 3D printing, internal wing geometry was manually designed and tested to reduce weight while ensuring structural integrity.

  • Optimized designs for weight reduction.
  • Ensured manufacturability with 3D printing constraints.
  • Performed structural analysis on critical components.

Slicer Fine-tuning

Video

Fine-tuned slicer parameters to obtain the best quality prints, e.g., flow rate, layer height, seam position, travel and layer speed, etc.

  • Adjusted print settings for optimal layer adhesion.
  • Reduced print time without compromising quality.
  • Resolved issues like warping and stringing.

Results

Areas our team performed specially well.

  • Internal wing geometry provided the team with a significant advantage over competitors, thanks to the extensive effort and investment made in optimizing a design that achieved the ideal balance between structural integrity and weight.
  • Placement and routing design of carbon tubes proved to be an effective method for minimizing printed supports, thereby reducing the overall weight.
  • Due to adequate balancing, our aircraft was able to maintain flight.
  • Modular design allowed for fast field repairs and part replacement.

Lessons learned & 2025

Highlights of the flight performance, showcasing the aircraft's capabilities and results achieved during the competition.

  • Torque generated by motor to fuselage structure should not be dismissed due to it altering flight trajectory.
  • Increase margin of error to 10% on project timeline to ensure printing complications don’t delay necessary test flights.
  • Invest in learning to print LW-PLA, weight difference is abysmal (1.24 g/cm3 vs. 0.54 g/cm^3).
