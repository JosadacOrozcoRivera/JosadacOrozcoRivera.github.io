CSU 3D-Printed Fixed-Wing Aircraft Competition (2023, 2024, 2025)

Aircraft Design & Manufacturing Lead.

Description

3DPAC is a aeronautical design competition that takes place in the State LA campus, where undergraduate students are challenged with designing, building and flying a 3D printed aircraft. The planes need to be lightweight, and the designs need to maximize performance given manufacturing and material constraints.

My Contribution

Team Leader

Led a team of six students from various fields, including mechanical engineering and mechatronics, in organizing the research, design, manufacturing, and testing stages to make the best use of the team’s skills and time. Also focused on managing resources effectively and finding ways to promote the project to attract additional sponsors and support.

Aicraft Dimensioning & Aerodesign

Created the first draft of how aircraft would be sized, applying aeronautical fundamentals to ensure wing geometry generated the most lift. Iterated using XFLR5 to obtain the most efficient airfoil.

CAD Design for Additive Manufacturing

Designed the aircraft in Solidworks, including wings, tail section, and fuselage. Due to the nature of 3D printing, internal wing geometry was manually designed and tested to reduce weight while ensuring structural integrity.

Slicer Fine-tuning

Fine-tuned Prusaslicer parameters to obtain the best quality prints, e.g., flow rate, layer height, seam position, travel and layer speed, etc.

Results

The project successfully produced a lightweight, efficient aircraft that performed exceptionally well during competition, achieving top rankings in both design and flight performance categories. The team also gained valuable experience in aeronautical design and additive manufacturing techniques.
